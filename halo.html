1. Haloalkanes (Alkyl Halides – RX) – Reactions & Mechanisms

SN2 – Nucleophilic Substitution

RX + Nu⁻ → RNu + X⁻

Favored: 1° > 2° >> 3° (steric), strong Nu, polar aprotic solvent, inversion of configuration.

Mechanism (One-step, concerted):

  • Nu⁻ attacks from back side → transition state with partial bonds
  • Simultaneous C–Nu bond formation & C–X bond breaking
  • Inversion of configuration (umbrella flip) if chiral center
  • Rate = k [RX][Nu⁻] → bimolecular

Energy: One transition state, no intermediate.

SN1 – Nucleophilic Substitution

RX → R⁺ + X⁻ (slow) → RNu (fast)

Favored: 3° > 2° > 1°, weak Nu, polar protic solvent, racemization.

Mechanism (Two-step):

  1. Step 1 (RDS): Heterolytic cleavage → planar carbocation + X⁻
  2. Step 2: Nu attacks carbocation from either side → racemic mixture if chiral

Rate = k [RX] → unimolecular

Carbocation rearrangements possible (1,2-hydride/methyl shift).

E2 – Dehydrohalogenation (alc. KOH)

RX + alc. KOH → Alkene + KX + H₂O

Saytzeff rule: more substituted alkene major • Anti-periplanar geometry.

Mechanism (One-step, concerted):

  • Strong base (OH⁻) abstracts β-H
  • Leaving group (X) departs simultaneously
  • C=C π bond forms
  • Rate = k [RX][Base] → bimolecular

Anti-elimination preferred in acyclic systems.

Williamson Ether Synthesis

RX + NaOR' → R–O–R' + NaX

SN2 – best with 1° RX.

Ambident Nucleophiles (KCN vs AgCN)

RX + KCN → R–CN (nitrile)
RX + AgCN → R–NC (isocyanide)

CN⁻ ambident: C-attack (K⁺) vs N-attack (Ag⁺ hardens N).

Wurtz Reaction

2RX + 2Na → R–R + 2NaX (dry ether)

Free radical coupling via organosodium intermediate.

Grignard Reagent Formation

RX + Mg → RMgX (dry ether)

Radical mechanism on Mg surface → insertion.

2. Haloarenes (ArX) – Reactions & Mechanisms

Low Reactivity – Why?
  • Resonance: partial double bond character (C–X bond stronger, shorter)
  • sp² carbon: harder for backside attack
  • Unstable aryl carbocation → no SN1
  • No SN2 (geometry + resonance)
Nucleophilic Substitution (Harsh conditions)

ArX + NaOH (623 K, pressure) → ArOH

Mechanism (for activated ArX or high T/P):

  1. Nu⁻ adds to carbon bearing X → Meisenheimer complex (negative charge delocalized)
  2. Loss of X⁻ restores aromaticity

Requires electron-withdrawing groups ortho/para to X for easier addition.

Wurtz-Fittig & Fittig

ArX + RX + 2Na → Ar–R
2ArX + 2Na → Ar–Ar

Free radical via organosodium.
